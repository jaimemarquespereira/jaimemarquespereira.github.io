Work in Progress

Do TV News Chase Twitter? A High-Frequency Analysis.

Abstract: Using closed captioned transcripts, I analyze cable outlets coverage in narrow time intervals centered around President Trump's tweets. At first, comparing U.S. outlets' coverage with that of a relatively impartial BBC, I find that FNC seemed to decrease Trump coverage right after a generic tweet by President Trump while CNN and MSN seemed to be insensitive. In a second analysis, using a comprehensive dataset covering replies on Twitter to President Trump tweets, I weight each of Trump's tweets according to how large of a reaction these have ``instantaneously'' generated on Twitter. Here, I conclude that: (i) tweets that received a higher number of replies are associated with larger decreases/increases of Trump coverage from FNC, CNN and MSN; (ii) FNC seemed to decrease Trump coverage right after a tweet while CNN and MSN, contrary to initial results, tended to increase coverage after a generic tweet.
