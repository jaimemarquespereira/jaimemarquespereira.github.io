Work in Progress

Do TV News Chase Twitter? A High-Frequency Analysis.

Abstract: Using closed captioned transcripts, I analyze cable outlets coverage in narrow time intervals centered around President Trump's tweets. Allowing for year-specific responses for each network, I find that (i) cable news networks covered President Trump's tweets in real-time and (ii) networks' coverage became increasingly asymmetric over time. In a second analysis, using a comprehensive dataset covering replies on Twitter to President Trump's tweets, I weight tweets according to how large of a reaction these have ``instantaneously'' generated on Twitter. Here, I find similar results to those just described and then more: (i) networks' coverage became increasingly asymmetric over time and (ii) those tweets which generated larger ``instantaneous'' reactions on Twitter seem to be associated with larger coverage responses by cable outlets.
